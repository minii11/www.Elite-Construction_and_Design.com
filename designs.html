Detailed Design Styles

Modern: Characterized by clean lines, open spaces, and minimal ornamentation, modern homes emphasize functionality and simplicity.

Traditional: Traditional homes feature classic architectural details, warm colors, and cozy layouts, evoking a sense of nostalgia and comfort.

Rustic: Rustic designs highlight natural materials like wood and stone, promoting a cozy, countryside feel that blends seamlessly with nature.

Industrial: Inspired by warehouses and factories, industrial homes showcase raw materials, open spaces, and a neutral color palette for a chic, urban vibe.

Contemporary: Contemporary design changes with trends, often incorporating eco-friendly elements and innovative layouts that reflect current lifestyles.

Minimalist: Focusing on simplicity and functionality, minimalist homes feature a limited color palette, uncluttered spaces, and essential furnishings.

Mediterranean: Mediterranean homes are known for their stucco exteriors, red-tile roofs, and wrought iron details, evoking the charm of coastal living.

Victorian: Victorian architecture features intricate details, vibrant colors, and asymmetrical shapes, bringing a sense of grandeur and history.

Art Deco: Art Deco homes are characterized by bold geometric shapes, rich colors, and luxurious materials, reflecting the glamour of the 1920s.

Eco-Friendly: Eco-friendly designs prioritize sustainability with energy-efficient materials, solar panels, and sustainable construction practices.
